3. How is coaching different from therapy or consulting?

This is a common question people ask before starting executive coaching, and it is an important one. The three disciplines can seem similar, but they serve very different purposes.

Therapy tends to focus on the past. It helps people process experiences, work through psychological challenges, and heal. Consulting is about expertise: a consultant diagnoses a problem and hands you a recommended solution. Executive coaching is neither of those things.

Coaching is forward-focused and action-oriented. The goal is to help you get clear on what you actually want, identify aligned actions, build momentum, and generate results in your personal or professional life. I do not hand you a prescription. I work alongside you as we clarify your authentic desires, dissolve blocks, and identify your best next steps.

My approach is intuitive and insightful. In addition to a B.S. in Psychology and Business and over 20 years of experience, I have a rare ability to pick up on powerful nuance and subtlety.
